Florence Highlights

Start at Piazza del Duomo, where the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore dominates the skyline. Climb the dome for panoramic views, then wander toward Piazza della Signoria — the open-air museum of Florence. End your day at the Ponte Vecchio, a medieval bridge lined with small jewelry shops and a perfect sunset view.

What to Eat

Italian food in Florence goes beyond pizza and pasta. Try a panino from a local sandwich shop or a bowl of ribollita, a hearty Tuscan soup made with bread and vegetables. For dessert, treat yourself to authentic gelato — the creamy kind made in small batches. The best spots are usually away from the main tourist streets.

Rome Monuments

If your travels continue south, Rome awaits with its iconic landmarks: the Colosseum, Trevi Fountain, and Vatican City. The city mixes ruins and modern life effortlessly. Grab a cappuccino near the Pantheon and enjoy people-watching in the piazza.

Budget Tips for Students

  • Use free museum days (first Sunday of each month in Italy).
  • Book train tickets early using Trenitalia or Italo for discounts.
  • Stay in hostels or student housing for budget-friendly lodging.
  • Eat lunch like a local — restaurants offer affordable fixed-price menus.
