Top Attractions in Florence

Begin your exploration at the Duomo di Firenze, one of the world’s most magnificent cathedrals. Climb its 463 steps to enjoy breathtaking views of the city. Visit the Uffizi Gallery to admire masterpieces by Botticelli, Leonardo da Vinci, and Raphael. Don’t forget Ponte Vecchio, Florence’s oldest bridge, known for its glittering jewelry shops.

Beyond the big landmarks, explore smaller gems like the Mercato Centrale for fresh food, or the Oltrarno neighborhood, where artisans still make leather, paper, and jewelry by hand. Every corner tells a story.

Local Food and Dining

Italian meals are an experience, not just a necessity. Start your day with a cappuccino and pastry. Lunch often includes a light pasta or panino (sandwich), and dinner may feature Tuscan specialties like pappa al pomodoro or bistecca alla fiorentina. Remember: Italians rarely drink cappuccino after 11 a.m. and dinner usually starts around 8 p.m.

For budget travelers, bakeries and small trattorias offer “menu del giorno” — a fixed-price menu with a starter, main, and drink for a fraction of the cost of tourist restaurants.

Exploring Beyond Florence

Italy’s train network makes day trips easy. From Florence, you can reach:

  • Pisa — to climb the famous Leaning Tower.
  • Siena — to walk through medieval streets and visit Piazza del Campo.
  • Rome — Italy’s capital, full of ancient monuments and lively streets.

Rome Highlights

Rome offers layers of history. Stand in the Colosseum where gladiators once fought, throw a coin into the Trevi Fountain to ensure your return, and tour the Vatican Museums to see Michelangelo’s Sistine Chapel. Each district has its own charm — Trastevere is bohemian, Monti is trendy, and Centro Storico is pure history.

Student Travel Advice

  • Book train tickets early for cheaper prices and seat reservations.
  • Download offline maps before heading out.
  • Use refillable bottles — tap water in Italy is safe and eco-friendly.
  • Carry photocopies of your passport and student ID separately from the originals.
  • Visit small museums and churches — they’re often free and less crowded.
